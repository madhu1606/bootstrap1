HISTORY OF ART

INTRO

This article is about the general concept of art. For the group of creative disciplines, see The arts. For other uses, see Art (disambiguation). Art is a diverse range of (and products of) human activities involving creative imagination to express technical proficiency, beauty, emotional power, or conceptual ideas.[1][2][3]

There is no generally agreed definition of what constitutes art,
and ideas have changed over time. The three classical
branches of visual art are painting, sculpture, and architecture.
Theatre, dance, and other performing arts, as well as literature, music, film and other media
such as interactive media, are included in a broader definition of the arts.[1][8] Until the 17th century, art referred to any
skill or mastery and was not differentiated from crafts or sciences.
In modern usage after the 17th century, where aesthetic considerations are paramount, the fine arts are separated and
distinguished from acquired skills in general, such as the decorative or applied arts.

The nature of art and related concepts, such as creativity and interpretation,
are explored in a branch of philosophy known as aesthetics.
The resulting artworks are studied in
the professional fields of art criticism and the history of art.
